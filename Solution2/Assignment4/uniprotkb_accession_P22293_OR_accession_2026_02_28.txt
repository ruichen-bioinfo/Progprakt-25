CC       general cofactor required for essentially all RNA polymerase II
CC       transcription (PubMed:25216679, PubMed:28918903). At the promoters,
CC       SAGA is required for transcription pre-initiation complex (PIC)
CC       recruitment. It influences RNA polymerase II transcriptional activity
CC       through different activities such as TBP interaction (via core/TAF
CC       module) and promoter selectivity, interaction with transcription
CC       activators (via Tra1/SPT module), and chromatin modification through
CC       histone acetylation (via HAT module) and deubiquitination (via DUB
CC       module) (PubMed:16855026, PubMed:31969703). SAGA preferentially
CC       acetylates histones H3 (to form H3K9ac, H3K14ac, H3K18ac and H3K23ac)
CC       and H2B and deubiquitinates histone H2B (PubMed:20395473). SAGA
CC       interacts with DNA via upstream activating sequences (UASs)
CC       (PubMed:28918903, PubMed:29598828). Also identified in a modified
CC       version of SAGA named SALSA or SLIK. The cleavage of SPT7 and the
CC       absence of the SPT8 subunit in SLIK neither drive any major
CC       conformational differences in its structure compared with SAGA, nor
CC       significantly affect HAT, DUB, or DNA-binding activities
CC       (PubMed:33864814). Within the SAGA complex, participates in a
CC       subcomplex with SGF11, SGF73 and UBP8 required for deubiquitination of
CC       H2B and for the maintenance of steady-state H3 methylation levels
CC       (PubMed:16855026). The TREX-2 complex functions in docking export-
CC       competent ribonucleoprotein particles (mRNPs) to the nuclear entrance
CC       of the nuclear pore complex (nuclear basket), by association with
CC       components of the nuclear mRNA export machinery (MEX67-MTR2 and SUB2)
CC       in the nucleoplasm and the nucleoporin NUP1 at the nuclear basket.
CC       TREX-2 participates in mRNA export and accurate chromatin positioning
CC       in the nucleus by tethering genes to the nuclear periphery
CC       (PubMed:16760982, PubMed:18003937, PubMed:19328066). SUS1 also has a
CC       role in mRNP biogenesis and maintenance of genome integrity through
CC       preventing RNA-mediated genome instability (PubMed:18667528). Has a
CC       role in response to DNA damage induced by methyl methane sulfonate
CC       (MMS) and replication arrest induced by hydroxyurea (PubMed:16510898).

CC       module (GCN5, ADA2, NGG1/ADA3 and SGF29), the DUB (deubiquitinating)

CC       recruits TBP (TATA-binding protein), the HAT module contains the
CC       histone H3 acetyltransferase GCN5, and the DUB module comprises the
CC       histone H2B deubiquitinase UBP8 (PubMed:25216679, PubMed:31969703).
